Dr. Maurice Hilleman
(1919-2005)

Maurice Hilleman pictured here with a pea-tree dish.

A native of Montana who was born in Miles City in 1919. He created 40 vaccines -- many of which are still in use today.

Notable Achievements:

  • 1963 - Developed the vaccination for mumps after his daughter contracted the disease.
  • 1981 - Created the vaccination for hepatitis B, this was later withdrawn from the United States in 1986.

"If I had to name a person who has done more for the benefit of human health, with less recognition than anyone else, it would be Maurice Hilleman. -Robert Gallo
